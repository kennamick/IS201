14 Days Across Europe: Our January Adventure

This January, my husband and I went on a two-week journey through Europe, visiting Portugal, Spain, and the Netherlands. Traveling in the off-season was a blessing—quiet, peaceful, and full of discovery. If you're planning a winter trip to Europe, I hope our experience can help you prepare and get excited!

Lisbon, Portugal – History and Hills

Lisbon is full of color and character. We explored the Alfama district, visited Jerónimos Monastery, and enjoyed fresh pastéis de nata. Comfortable shoes are essential, and January brings some rain—so pack layers and a light umbrella.

Sintra – Castles in the Clouds

Sintra felt like a storybook. Pena Palace and Quinta da Regaleira were breathtaking, surrounded by misty gardens and forested hills. Dress warmly and bring snacks—it’s a dreamy place for daydreaming and exploring.

Leiria – A Peaceful Pause

Leiria was a refreshing slower-paced town with a lovely medieval castle. We watched the sunset by the riverside and enjoyed the local bakery scene. A great spot for resetting before the next adventure!

Porto – Tiled Beauty and Riverside Views

From the tiled buildings to the peaceful Douro River, Porto was packed with charm. We visited Livraria Lello, one of the prettiest bookstores I’ve ever seen, and ended our days with beautiful views. Bring a warm jacket and enjoy the local food scene!

Barcelona, Spain – Art, Architecture, and Awe

Gaudí's architecture blew us away—Sagrada Família and Park Güell were unforgettable. The Gothic Quarter was full of character and history. January made it easier to explore with fewer crowds, though we were glad to have packed warm layers.

Lagos, Portugal – Coastal Calm

Lagos was quiet and peaceful. We hiked along the cliffs at Ponta da Piedade and spent time by the sea. Many beachside shops were closed for the season, so we packed our own snacks and enjoyed a slower pace.

Amsterdam, Netherlands – A Short but Sweet Stop

Though we were only there a day, Amsterdam was lovely. We walked along the canals, explored local shops, and visited the Anne Frank House. Even in winter, it’s a walkable and welcoming city—just bundle up!

Packing for a Winter Adventure

Layering was key! I packed long sleeves, sweaters, a waterproof jacket, and scarves. We fit everything in carry-ons and made it work. January travel also meant fewer crowds and more relaxed moments.

Final Thoughts

This trip reminded me that travel doesn’t have to be rushed or glamorous to be meaningful. From quiet cathedrals to windswept beaches, we found joy in simple, sacred moments. If you’re thinking of traveling in winter, I highly recommend it.
